FAQ

What is Sora?

Sora is an AI model that can create realistic and imaginative scenes from text instructions.

How to get access to Sora?

There is no way to request access at the moment.For the latest info on Sora, please refer to: https://openai.com/sora

How to use Sora?

At present, the Sora model developed by OpenAI is not available for public use. Nevertheless, users have the opportunity to experience it indirectly by leaving a video prompt (described in text form) in the comment section of Sam Altman's Twitter feed. Sam Altman, who leads OpenAI, might then select your prompt to generate and post a video created by the Sora model. While this method does not offer direct access to Sora, it provides a glimpse into its capabilities. For the latest news and details regarding Sora, consider following Sam Altman on Twitter.

Who gets to use Sora by OpenAI?

Sora by OpenAI is not yet publicly available and is currently in use by select groups. These include red teamers, who are cybersecurity experts working to secure the AI against misuse, and a handpicked group of content creators exploring its creative possibilities. OpenAI is using their insights to prepare Sora for wider use, focusing on safety and ethical application. To stay updated on Sora’s public release, follow OpenAI’s communications. The approach towards Sora’s launch emphasizes careful preparation and responsible innovation.

Will OpenAI's Sora be open-sourced?

The likelihood is quite low. With OpenAI's shift from a non-profit to a for-profit entity, its AI offerings are moving increasingly towards commercial models.
